About

I am a robotics-focused engineer with a strong interdisciplinary background in mechanical engineering, applied mathematics, and computer science. I graduated from the University of Massachusetts Amherst with three bachelor’s degrees and a minor in business, which has given me both technical depth and a broad systems-level perspective.

My interest in robotics began in high school through participation in the BOTBALL robotics competition. Designing and building autonomous robots from LEGO and metal components sparked my curiosity about how machines can perceive, reason, and act in the physical world. That experience motivated me to pursue the technical foundation needed to build real-world robotic systems.

Through my academic training, I have developed hands-on experience with CAD, finite element analysis using ANSYS, and core engineering design principles. My coursework and projects span mechanical design, numerical methods, algorithms, and software development, allowing me to approach problems from both hardware and software perspectives.

I have also gained industry experience as an intern at Amazon, working on AWS Redshift, where I contributed to large-scale systems in a production environment. This experience strengthened my skills in software engineering, collaboration, and building reliable tools at scale.

My long-term goal is to advance the field of robotics through research while also teaching and mentoring others. I am deeply motivated by the idea of making robotics more accessible and inspiring the next generation of engineers—just as I was inspired to pursue this field.
